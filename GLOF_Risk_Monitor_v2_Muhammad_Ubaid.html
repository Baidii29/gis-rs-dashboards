Remote Sensing Methodology
This dashboard uses spectral indices derived from multispectral satellite imagery to detect, monitor, and classify glacial lakes and ice cover across the HKH region. The three indices below are the standard analytical tools used in international GLOF risk assessment research and are referenced in ICIMOD and NASA glaciology studies.
🌊
NDWI
Normalized Difference Water Index
NDWI = (Green − NIR) / (Green + NIR)
Sentinel-2: (B3 − B8) / (B3 + B8)  ·  Landsat-8: (B3 − B5) / (B3 + B5)
Shishper Lake — Current Value0.68
NDWI highlights open water bodies by exploiting the strong contrast between green light reflectance and near-infrared absorption by water. Values range from −1 to +1. Values above 0.3 indicate water presence; above 0.5 indicates large open water bodies.
Application in GLOF ResearchUsed to precisely map glacial lake boundaries and track seasonal and annual expansion. A rising NDWI value in a monitored lake indicates increasing water volume — a key early warning signal for GLOF risk escalation.
❄️
NDSI
Normalized Difference Snow Index
NDSI = (Green − SWIR) / (Green + SWIR)
Sentinel-2: (B3 − B11) / (B3 + B11)  ·  Landsat-8: (B3 − B6) / (B3 + B6)
HKH Region — Seasonal Average0.74
NDSI differentiates snow and ice from other land cover. Snow and ice strongly reflect green wavelengths but absorb shortwave infrared (SWIR). Values above 0.4 confirm seasonal snow cover; values above 0.6 indicate permanent glacier ice.
Application in GLOF ResearchTracks glacier surface area and ice cover seasonality over time. Decreasing NDSI trends confirm glacier retreat, which weakens natural ice dams, reduces moraine stability, and directly elevates GLOF outburst flood risk.
🌿
NDVI
Normalized Difference Vegetation Index
NDVI = (NIR − Red) / (NIR + Red)
Sentinel-2: (B8 − B4) / (B8 + B4)  ·  Landsat-8: (B5 − B4) / (B5 + B4)
Glacial Zone — Terrain Average0.12
NDVI measures the density of live green vegetation. Values range from −1 (bare rock/water/ice) to +1 (dense forest). In glacial high-altitude zones, values near zero confirm barren rocky or ice terrain with no vegetation buffer.
Application in GLOF ResearchLow NDVI confirms glaciated terrain with no vegetation to slow floodwaters. Post-GLOF analysis uses NDVI change detection to map landscape damage, vegetation loss, and soil erosion in downstream flood corridors.
Satellite Platforms Used
Three primary satellite missions provide the imagery used in this GLOF monitoring dashboard. Each offers different spatial resolution and temporal frequency characteristics suited to specific analysis tasks.
Landsat 8 / 9
USGS / NASA — USA
30m multispectral resolution with a continuous archive stretching back to 1972, enabling 50+ year glacial lake change detection. The OLI (Operational Land Imager) sensor provides 11 spectral bands ideal for computing NDWI, NDSI, and NDVI across Pakistan's mountain regions.

10–60m multispectral imagery with higher spatial resolution than Landsat for precise lake boundary delineation. The MSI (Multispectral Instrument) sensor with 13 spectral bands and 5-day revisit time enables near-real-time glacial lake monitoring during critical melt seasons.

Scientific Background
What is a Glacial Lake Outburst Flood?
A Glacial Lake Outburst Flood (GLOF) occurs when a natural dam — formed by ice, moraine debris, or bedrock — holding back a glacial lake suddenly fails catastrophically. The resulting flood releases millions of cubic meters of water, ice, and debris within hours, traveling at extreme speeds down mountain valleys and destroying roads, bridges, villages, and farmland with devastating force.
🇵🇰
Why Pakistan is the World's Most GLOF-Vulnerable Country
  • Pakistan contains over 7,253 glaciers — more than anywhere outside the polar regions
  • The HKH region holds 3,044 glacial lakes, of which 33 are classified as critical danger zones
  • HKH temperatures are rising at 3× the global average — accelerating glacier melt faster than anywhere on Earth
  • Mountain communities have very limited early warning systems and extremely difficult terrain for emergency evacuation
  • Between 1959 and 2020, Pakistan experienced over 150 recorded GLOF events causing massive loss of life and infrastructure damage
  • In 2022 alone, over 170 GLOF events struck Pakistan during the catastrophic monsoon flood season
⚙️
GLOF Dam Types and Failure Mechanisms
  • Ice-dammed lakes: Formed when a glacier blocks a valley. Highly unstable — the ice dam can melt rapidly from beneath during warm seasons causing sudden failure
  • Moraine-dammed lakes: Retained by unstable glacial rock and debris deposits. Prone to collapse from water overtopping, seepage erosion, or earthquake shaking
  • Bedrock-dammed lakes: More stable but still vulnerable when rapid ice melt raises water levels beyond the dam's containment capacity
  • Triggering factors: Heavy rainfall events, seismic activity, ice/rock avalanche impact, or rapid temperature spikes can all initiate sudden dam failure
  • Early warning signs: Rapid lake level rise, increased water turbidity, cracking sounds from ice dam, unusual seismic tremors near the lake
